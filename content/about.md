I’m a software engineer who has spent a significant part of my career working on enterprise applications, backend systems, distributed architectures, and the kind of engineering problems that usually live behind APIs, logs, dashboards, and production systems.

Most of my interests revolve around:

- Java and the JVM ecosystem,
- backend and distributed systems design,
- observability and tracing,
- concurrency and performance,
- cloud-native architectures,
- and understanding how systems behave under real-world conditions.

Alongside professional work, I also spend time exploring independent technical ideas and building side projects that allow me to experiment, learn, and think beyond day-to-day delivery work.

This blog primarily serves as a personal knowledge archive — a place where I document concepts, debugging journeys, architectural explorations, learning notes, and technical questions encountered while studying and revisiting different areas of software engineering.

The posts here are influenced by:

 - technical books,
- official documentation,
- experiments,
- production debugging,
- discussions with peers,
- research across blogs, papers, and forums,
- and occasionally AI-assisted explorations used as part of the learning process.

Many of the articles are intentionally exploratory in nature, capturing the process of understanding itself — the confusions, trade-offs, design questions, and mental models that emerge while learning complex systems. At the same time, some posts may evolve into more deeply researched and opinionated pieces developed through experimentation, synthesis of multiple sources, and practical engineering experience.

This blog is therefore both:

- a long-term learning archive,
- and an evolving attempt to develop clearer thinking and better technical writing over time.
